What Is the IRS Fresh Start Program?

The IRS Fresh Start Program is an umbrella term for a series of policy changes the IRS made starting in 2011 to make it easier for struggling taxpayers to resolve tax debt. It expanded eligibility for installment agreements, made Offers in Compromise more accessible, and created new lien relief options. The program is ongoing and continues to evolve.

📋 What Fresh Start Changed
Raised OIC qualification threshold, extended payment plan time to 72 months, increased lien filing threshold to $10,000, added lien withdrawal for Direct Debit agreements, and streamlined CNC qualifications.

The 4 Major Components of Fresh Start

1. Expanded Offer in Compromise Eligibility

Before Fresh Start, the IRS calculated your future income potential over 4 years (for lump-sum offers). Fresh Start reduced it to 1 year. This dramatically lowered the minimum offer amount, making OICs accessible to more taxpayers.

2. Streamlined Installment Agreements

Fresh Start raised the streamlined agreement limit from $25,000 to $50,000, allowing more taxpayers to set up plans without extensive financial disclosure. The maximum repayment period was also extended from 60 to 72 months.

3. Tax Lien Relief

Fresh Start raised the threshold for automatic lien filing from $5,000 to $10,000. It also created two new lien withdrawal options:

  • Withdrawal after entering a Direct Debit Installment Agreement
  • Withdrawal after the lien is released (to help with credit recovery)

4. Self-Employed Penalty Relief

Fresh Start provided targeted penalty relief for self-employed individuals who were unemployed or underemployed during 2011–2012. Similar relief has been extended in various forms since.

Does Fresh Start Mean Automatic Forgiveness?

No. Fresh Start is not an amnesty program. You still have to apply for each resolution option and qualify based on your financial situation. However, Fresh Start significantly lowered the bar for qualification, meaning many taxpayers who didn't qualify before now can.

How to Access Fresh Start Programs

There is no single "Fresh Start application." You apply for the specific resolution:

  • OIC — File Forms 656 and 433-A
  • Installment Agreement — IRS Online Payment Agreement or Form 9465
  • Lien Withdrawal — Form 12277
  • CNC Status — Submit Form 433-F with financial documentation
