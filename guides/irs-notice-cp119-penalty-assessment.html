What Is IRS Notice CP119?

IRS Notice CP119 is a penalty assessment notice. It informs you that the IRS has added one or more penalties to your tax account — most commonly for failure to make required estimated tax payments, failure to deposit payroll taxes, or other compliance failures. The notice shows you the penalty amount, the reason, and the total new balance owed.

📋 CP119 vs CP14
CP119 is specifically about a penalty being added — it's not the same as the initial balance due notice (CP14). You may receive both for the same tax year: CP14 for the underlying tax, and CP119 for additional penalties assessed.

Can CP119 Penalties Be Removed?

Yes — many CP119 penalties can be reduced or eliminated through:

First-Time Penalty Abatement (FTA)

If you have a clean compliance record for the prior 3 years (no penalties), the IRS will typically waive the penalty upon request — no documentation required. This applies to most common penalties assessed via CP119.

Reasonable Cause

If the penalty resulted from circumstances beyond your control — illness, natural disaster, reliance on incorrect professional advice — you can request abatement with supporting documentation.

Statutory Exception

If the penalty was triggered by an IRS error or incorrect written advice from the IRS, it must be removed.

✅ Penalty Abatement Is Underused
Most taxpayers simply pay CP119 penalties without asking for removal. A simple call or letter requesting First-Time Abatement often results in immediate penalty removal — saving hundreds or thousands of dollars. Always ask before paying.

How to Respond to CP119

  1. Review the penalty type and calculation for accuracy
  2. Determine if you qualify for First-Time Abatement or Reasonable Cause
  3. Call the IRS penalty line (listed on the notice) or have a tax professional call
  4. If requesting in writing, use Form 843 (Claim for Refund and Request for Abatement)
  5. If the balance remains after abatement, establish a payment plan
