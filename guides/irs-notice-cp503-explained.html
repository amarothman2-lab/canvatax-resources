IRS Notice CP503: Final Warning Before Collections

⚠️ This is your third and final reminder. The IRS has contacted you twice already (CP14 and CP501). If you do not act now, the next step is CP504 — the Notice of Intent to Levy your wages and bank accounts.

What Is CP503?

CP503 is the third notice in the IRS escalation sequence. At this stage, the IRS has been patient. This notice signals that collection enforcement is imminent.

What Happens If You Do Nothing?

  • ❌ CP504 is issued — legal authorization to seize your assets begins
  • ❌ State tax refund intercepted immediately
  • ❌ Wage garnishment — up to 70% of every paycheck
  • ❌ Bank account levied — all funds frozen and seized
  • ❌ Federal tax lien placed on your home and property
  • ❌ Passport revoked for debts over $62,000

Why CP503 Is Your Best Window

Taxpayers who resolve their debt at the CP503 stage have significantly more options than those who wait for CP504. Before enforcement begins, the IRS is more flexible and more willing to negotiate favorable terms.
