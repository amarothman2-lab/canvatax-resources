What Is IRS Notice CP504?

IRS Notice CP504 is titled "Notice of Intent to Levy" and is one of the most serious IRS notices you can receive. At the CP504 stage, the IRS has legal authority to seize your state tax refund and is preparing to file a federal tax lien against your assets. This is the final notice before the IRS escalates to wage garnishment and bank account levies.

🚨 CP504 Is a Legal Threat — Not a Reminder
Unlike earlier notices (CP14, CP501, CP503), CP504 carries immediate legal consequences. The IRS can seize your state tax refund without further notice. A federal tax lien filing may be imminent. Call (858) 610-9779 right now.

What CP504 Authorizes the IRS to Do

  • Seize your state tax refund — immediately, without additional notice
  • File a Notice of Federal Tax Lien — making your debt public and damaging credit
  • Escalate to wage garnishment — if you don't respond, LT11 is next
  • Levy your bank accounts — after issuing the final LT11 notice

How Much Time Do You Have?

CP504 gives you 30 days before the IRS can issue the full levy notice (LT11). After LT11, you have another 30 days. That means you have roughly 30–60 days before your wages or bank accounts are at risk — but your state refund can be seized immediately.

Your Best Options Right Now

1. Set Up a Payment Plan (Fastest Protection)

Call the IRS, set up an installment agreement, and the levy sequence stops. A tax professional can do this in one call and prevent all further enforcement.

2. Submit an Offer in Compromise

Filing an OIC pauses ALL collection activity — including the levy sequence and lien filing — while the IRS reviews your offer.

3. Request a Collection Due Process (CDP) Hearing

File Form 12153 within 30 days of the CP504 date. This triggers a formal hearing and legally stops the IRS from levying while the hearing is pending. This buys critical time.
