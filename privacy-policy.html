Privacy Policy

Last Updated: April 23, 2026

CanvaTax ("we," "our," or "us") is committed to protecting the privacy and security of your personal information. This Privacy Policy describes how we collect, use, disclose, and safeguard your information when you visit our website, use our services, or contact us.

By using our website or services, you agree to the terms of this Privacy Policy. If you do not agree, please do not use our website or services.

1. Information We Collect

1.1 Information You Provide

When you contact us for a consultation or use our services, we may collect:

  • Full name
  • Phone number
  • Email address
  • Mailing address
  • Tax-related information (tax debt amount, tax years affected, IRS notices received)
  • Financial information necessary to evaluate your case (income, expenses, assets)
  • Employment status
  • Any other information you voluntarily provide

1.2 Information Collected Automatically

When you visit our website, we may automatically collect:

  • IP address
  • Browser type and version
  • Operating system
  • Pages visited and time spent
  • Referring website
  • Device information

1.3 Cookies and Tracking Technologies

We use cookies and similar technologies to improve your browsing experience, analyze website traffic, and personalize content. You can control cookie settings through your browser preferences.

2. How We Use Your Information

We use the information we collect to:

  • Provide tax resolution and relief services
  • Evaluate your eligibility for IRS programs (Offer in Compromise, installment agreements, penalty abatement, etc.)
  • Communicate with you about your case and our services
  • Respond to your inquiries and requests
  • Improve our website and services
  • Comply with legal and regulatory obligations
  • Protect against fraud and unauthorized activity

3. How We Protect Your Information

We implement industry-standard security measures to protect your personal information, including:

  • SSL/TLS encryption for all data transmitted through our website
  • Secure storage of sensitive financial and tax information
  • Access controls limiting who can view your data
  • Regular security assessments and updates

While we strive to protect your information, no method of transmission over the internet is 100% secure. We cannot guarantee absolute security.

4. Information Sharing and Disclosure

We do not sell, rent, or trade your personal information to third parties for marketing purposes.

We may share your information only in the following circumstances:

  • With the IRS or state tax authorities — only as necessary to represent you and resolve your tax issue, and only with your explicit written authorization (Power of Attorney, Form 2848)
  • With service providers — trusted third parties who assist us in operating our website and conducting our business, subject to confidentiality agreements
  • As required by law — to comply with legal obligations, court orders, or government requests
  • To protect rights — to enforce our terms of service or protect the safety of our users

5. Your Rights and Choices

You have the right to:

  • Access — Request a copy of the personal information we hold about you
  • Correction — Request correction of inaccurate or incomplete information
  • Deletion — Request deletion of your personal information (subject to legal retention requirements)
  • Opt-out — Opt out of marketing communications at any time
  • Withdraw consent — Withdraw your consent for data processing where applicable

To exercise any of these rights, contact us at support@canvatax.com or call (858) 610-9779.

6. California Privacy Rights (CCPA)

If you are a California resident, you have additional rights under the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA), including the right to know what personal information is collected, request deletion, and opt out of the sale of personal information. We do not sell personal information.

7. Data Retention

We retain your personal information for as long as necessary to fulfill the purposes outlined in this Privacy Policy, comply with legal obligations, resolve disputes, and enforce agreements. Tax-related records may be retained for a minimum of seven (7) years as required by IRS regulations and professional standards.

8. Third-Party Links

Our website may contain links to third-party websites. We are not responsible for the privacy practices of these external sites. We encourage you to review their privacy policies before providing personal information.

9. Children's Privacy

Our services are not directed to individuals under the age of 18. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children. If we learn that we have collected information from a child under 18, we will take steps to delete it promptly.

10. Changes to This Privacy Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. Changes will be posted on this page with an updated "Last Updated" date. We encourage you to review this policy periodically.
