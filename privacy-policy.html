1. Introduction & Scope

CanvaTax ("Company," "we," "our," or "us") is committed to protecting your privacy. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, disclose, and safeguard your information when you visit canvatax.com, blog.canvatax.com, and any subdomains or related websites (collectively, the "Sites"), or when you contact us regarding our IRS tax debt relief services.

By using our Sites or services, you acknowledge that you have read, understood, and agree to the data practices described in this Privacy Policy. If you do not agree, please do not use our Sites or services.

⚠️ Financial Information Notice: Because our services involve sensitive financial and tax matters, we take extraordinary care in protecting any information you share with us. All data is treated as confidential.

2. Information We Collect

2.1 Information You Provide Directly

  • Identity information: Full name, date of birth
  • Contact information: Email address, phone number, mailing address, state of residence
  • Financial and tax information: IRS debt amount, tax years affected, IRS notice numbers, employment status, income information, assets and liabilities
  • Communications: Messages, inquiries, consultation notes, and feedback you send to us
  • Form submissions: Lead forms, contact forms, and consultation request forms on our Sites

2.2 Information Collected Automatically

  • Log data: IP address, browser type and version, pages visited, time spent on pages, referring URLs, exit pages
  • Device information: Device type, operating system, screen resolution, language settings
  • Cookies and tracking: Session identifiers, preference data, analytics data (see Section 5)
  • Usage data: Clickstream data, search terms used on our Sites, features accessed

2.3 Information from Third Parties

  • Business contact information from publicly available sources
  • Referral information from partner organizations (with your consent)
  • Analytics data from Google Analytics and similar services

3. How We Use Your Information

We use the information we collect to:

  • Provide, operate, and improve our IRS tax debt relief services
  • Respond to your inquiries and fulfill consultation requests
  • Contact you about your case, next steps, or relevant IRS program changes
  • Analyze website usage to improve our content and user experience
  • Send you relevant educational content about IRS programs (you may opt-out at any time)
  • Comply with applicable laws, regulations, and legal obligations
  • Detect, prevent, and address fraud, security, or technical issues
  • Enforce our Terms of Service and other agreements

Legal Basis: We process your information based on (a) your consent, (b) the necessity of performing services you requested, (c) our legitimate business interests, and (d) compliance with legal obligations.

4. How We Share Your Information

We do NOT sell your personal information to third parties. We may share your information only in the following circumstances:

  • Service providers: Trusted third parties who assist with our operations (CRM systems, email platforms, analytics providers, cloud hosting) — bound by confidentiality agreements
  • Enrolled Agents and tax professionals: Licensed representatives who work on your case, subject to professional confidentiality obligations
  • Legal compliance: When required by law, court order, subpoena, or to cooperate with government investigations
  • Business transfers: In connection with a merger, acquisition, or sale of assets — you will be notified
  • With your consent: Any other sharing only with your explicit consent

🛡️ We never sell, rent, or trade your personal or financial information to data brokers, marketing companies, or any third parties for their own commercial purposes.

5. Cookies & Tracking Technologies

We use cookies and similar technologies to improve your experience on our Sites.

Types of Cookies We Use:

  • Essential cookies: Required for the Sites to function. Cannot be disabled.
  • Analytics cookies: Help us understand how visitors use our Sites (Google Analytics). Data is aggregated and anonymized.
  • Preference cookies: Remember your settings and preferences.
  • Marketing cookies: Used to deliver relevant information about our services to you across the web. You may opt-out.

Opting Out: You may disable cookies through your browser settings. Note that disabling cookies may affect site functionality. You may also opt out of Google Analytics by visiting Google's opt-out page.

6. Data Security

We implement industry-standard security measures to protect your information, including:

  • SSL/TLS encryption for all data transmitted to and from our Sites
  • Encrypted storage of sensitive financial and personal information
  • Access controls limiting who can view client data
  • Regular security audits and vulnerability assessments
  • Staff training on data privacy and confidentiality

Despite these measures, no method of transmission over the internet or electronic storage is 100% secure. We cannot guarantee absolute security but commit to notifying you promptly if a breach affects your data, as required by applicable law.

7. Data Retention

We retain your personal information for as long as necessary to:

  • Provide the services you requested
  • Comply with tax recordkeeping requirements (typically 7 years)
  • Resolve disputes and enforce agreements
  • Meet any applicable legal retention requirements

When data is no longer needed, we securely delete or anonymize it. You may request earlier deletion of your data subject to our legal obligations.

9. California Privacy Rights (CCPA/CPRA)

If you are a California resident, you have specific rights under the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA) and the California Privacy Rights Act (CPRA), effective January 1, 2023:

Your California Rights:

  • Right to Know: You have the right to know what categories and specific pieces of personal information we have collected about you in the past 12 months, the categories of sources, the business purpose for collection, and the categories of third parties with whom we share it.
  • Right to Delete: You have the right to request deletion of personal information we have collected, subject to certain exceptions.
  • Right to Correct: You have the right to request correction of inaccurate personal information.
  • Right to Opt-Out of Sale/Sharing: We do NOT sell or share personal information as defined under CCPA/CPRA. No opt-out is required.
  • Right to Limit Use of Sensitive Personal Information: You have the right to limit the use of sensitive personal information (including financial data) to what is necessary to provide our services.
  • Right to Non-Discrimination: We will not discriminate against you for exercising any of your CCPA rights.

Categories of Personal Information Collected (Last 12 Months):

  • Identifiers (name, email, phone, IP address)
  • Financial information (IRS debt amounts, tax information)
  • Commercial information (services requested)
  • Internet activity (browsing history on our Sites)
  • Geolocation data (state/city level only)

To submit a verifiable CCPA request, call us at (858) 610-9779 or contact us through canvatax.com. We will verify your identity before processing the request and respond within 45 days.
