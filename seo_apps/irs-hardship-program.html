What CNC Status Does For You

✅ Stops Wage Garnishment

Once CNC status is approved, any active wage garnishment must stop immediately. Your full paycheck is protected.

✅ Stops Bank Levies

The IRS cannot seize funds from your bank account while your account is in Currently Not Collectible status.

✅ No Monthly Payments

Unlike an installment agreement, CNC status requires no monthly payments. You pay nothing while the status is active.

✅ Buys Time to Recover

CNC status gives you breathing room to stabilize your finances. The 10-year collection statute continues to run — reducing your exposure.

✅ Automatic Review Periods

The IRS reviews your status periodically. If your income doesn't increase significantly, CNC can remain in place for years.

✅ Preserves OIC Rights

Being in CNC doesn't disqualify you from later filing an Offer in Compromise — you keep all settlement options open.

IRS Hardship Program: Complete Guide

What Is Currently Not Collectible Status?

Currently Not Collectible (CNC) is an official IRS designation for taxpayers who have no disposable income available to pay their tax debt after necessary living expenses. When the IRS places your account in CNC status, they suspend all collection activity — garnishments, levies, lien enforcement — for as long as the status remains active.

Who Qualifies for IRS Hardship Status?

You may qualify if your monthly income minus necessary living expenses leaves no money available for IRS payments. The IRS uses national and local expense standards to determine what "necessary" means — housing, food, transportation, healthcare, and minimum payments on other debts.

  • Unemployed or recently laid off
  • Fixed income (Social Security, disability) with high living costs
  • Medical emergency or catastrophic expense
  • Single parents with multiple dependents
  • Self-employed with declining business income

Does CNC Eliminate My Tax Debt?

No — CNC pauses collection, it doesn't erase the debt. However, the IRS has a 10-year statute of limitations on collection. Every year in CNC status is a year the clock runs. For many taxpayers, a combination of CNC status and eventual penalty abatement or an Offer in Compromise results in the debt being fully resolved for much less than the original balance.

How to Apply for IRS Hardship Status

You must submit a complete financial disclosure to the IRS — either Form 433-A (for individuals) or Form 433-F. The IRS reviews your income, expenses, and assets. If approved, they send written confirmation that collection activity has been suspended.

Working with a tax professional significantly improves your chances of approval — both by ensuring the financial disclosure is accurate and by presenting your expenses in the most favorable way within IRS guidelines.
