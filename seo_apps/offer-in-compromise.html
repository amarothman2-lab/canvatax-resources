IRS Offer in Compromise: The Complete Guide

What Is the IRS Offer in Compromise Program?

The Offer in Compromise (OIC) is an official IRS program established under Internal Revenue Code Section 7122. It allows taxpayers to settle their entire federal tax debt — including back taxes, penalties, and interest — for less than the full amount owed. Once an OIC is accepted and the agreed amount is paid, the tax debt is permanently extinguished.

How Does the IRS Calculate the Minimum Offer?

The IRS uses a specific formula: your Reasonable Collection Potential (RCP). This equals your monthly disposable income multiplied by either 12 (lump sum payment within 5 months) or 24 (periodic payments over 6–24 months), plus the net equity in your assets.

For example: if you have $150/month in disposable income and $3,000 in asset equity, your minimum offer for a lump sum payment would be approximately $4,800 — even if you owe $100,000.

Why Professional Help Matters for OIC

The IRS accepts roughly 40% of all OIC applications — but that rate is significantly higher with professional representation. The reasons offers fail are almost always avoidable: incorrect offer amounts, incomplete financial disclosures, missing documentation, or failure to respond to IRS requests during the review period. An experienced tax professional knows how to present your case in the most favorable light possible.
