What Is the IRS Fresh Start Program?

The IRS Fresh Start Program is an expanded set of policies designed to help struggling taxpayers resolve IRS debt more easily. Launched in 2011 and expanded multiple times since, Fresh Start isn't a single program — it's an umbrella covering several resolution options with more flexible eligibility than ever before.

What Fresh Start Covers

  • Offer in Compromise (OIC): The IRS loosened the financial formula used to calculate minimum offers. More taxpayers now qualify, and for lower amounts. The OIC lets you settle your entire balance for less than you owe — often 10–20 cents on the dollar.
  • Streamlined Installment Agreements: Fresh Start raised the threshold for no-review payment plans from $25,000 to $50,000. Under $50,000? Set up a plan online in minutes with no financial disclosure required.
  • Tax Lien Relief: The IRS raised the automatic lien threshold from $5,000 to $10,000, and now allows lien withdrawal after entering a Direct Debit installment agreement — protecting your credit and property title.
  • Penalty Relief: Expanded abatement options for taxpayers facing unemployment or significant income drops.

Who Qualifies for IRS Fresh Start?

  • You owe IRS tax debt you cannot pay in full
  • All required tax returns are filed and current
  • No open bankruptcy proceedings
  • For OIC: your assets + future income are less than what you owe

How to Apply

Each Fresh Start program has its own application process. Installment agreements can be set up at IRS.gov. Offers in Compromise require Form 656, Form 433-A, financial documentation, and a $205 application fee (waived for low-income filers).

The IRS reviews roughly 60,000 OIC applications per year and accepts about 40% — significantly more with professional representation.
