What Is an Offer in Compromise?

An Offer in Compromise (OIC) is an official IRS program that allows qualifying taxpayers to permanently settle their entire tax debt for less than the full amount owed. Once accepted, the remaining balance is legally forgiven — and the IRS releases all liens associated with the settled debt.

Who Qualifies?

The IRS accepts an OIC when your offer represents the maximum they can realistically collect given your financial situation. Key factors:

  • Your total assets (equity in home, car, savings, retirement) minus allowable expenses
  • Your monthly disposable income (income minus IRS-allowable living expenses)
  • All required returns must be filed
  • You must not be in an open bankruptcy
  • You must be current on estimated tax payments (if self-employed)

The OIC Formula — Your Reasonable Collection Potential

The IRS won't accept a penny less than what they calculate they can collect. Your minimum offer = net asset equity + (monthly disposable income × 12 for lump sum, or × 24 for periodic payment). If your offer meets or exceeds this number, the IRS must seriously consider it.

The Application Process

  1. Complete Form 656 (Offer in Compromise application)
  2. Complete Form 433-A OIC (individual financial statement) or 433-B OIC (business)
  3. Pay $205 application fee (waived for qualifying low-income applicants)
  4. Submit 20% of your lump-sum offer (or first month's payment for periodic)
  5. Wait 6–18 months for IRS review — all collection suspended during this period

After Acceptance

Once your OIC is accepted, you must remain in full tax compliance for 5 years — file all returns on time, pay all taxes due. Defaulting reinstates your original balance.
