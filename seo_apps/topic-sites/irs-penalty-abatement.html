What Are IRS Tax Penalties?

The IRS charges penalties on top of the underlying tax — and on a large debt, penalties can represent 25–50% of your total balance. The two most common:

  • Failure-to-File Penalty: 5% of unpaid tax per month, maximum 25%. Charged when you don't file your return by the deadline (including extensions).
  • Failure-to-Pay Penalty: 0.5% of unpaid tax per month, maximum 25%. Charged every month your balance remains unpaid. Reduced to 0.25%/month while in an installment agreement.
  • Accuracy-Related Penalty: 20% of underpayment resulting from negligence or substantial understatement of income.

First-Time Penalty Abatement (FTA)

The most powerful and underused tool in tax resolution. If you have a clean compliance history — no penalties assessed in the prior 3 tax years — the IRS will automatically remove your first year of penalties. No reason required. No documentation needed. Just ask.

You can request FTA by calling the IRS at 1-800-829-1040 or by submitting a written request. This can eliminate $10,000–$20,000+ in penalties on a single call.

Reasonable Cause Abatement

If FTA doesn't apply (you had prior penalties), you can still request abatement by documenting circumstances beyond your control:

  • Serious illness or hospitalization of yourself or immediate family member
  • Natural disaster affecting your ability to file or pay
  • Death of an immediate family member
  • Reliance on incorrect written advice from the IRS or a qualified tax professional
  • Significant business disruption (fire, theft, records destruction)

Statutory Exceptions

Certain penalties are automatically waived in specific circumstances — for example, estimated tax penalties are waived if your withholding meets the prior-year safe harbor rules.
