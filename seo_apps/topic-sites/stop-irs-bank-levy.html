What Is an IRS Bank Levy?

An IRS bank levy is a legal seizure of funds in your bank account. Unlike a wage garnishment (which is ongoing), a bank levy is a one-time action — but it freezes your entire account balance up to the amount owed.

The 21-Day Window — Act Immediately

When the IRS issues a bank levy, your bank is required by law to hold the funds for 21 days before sending them to the IRS. This 21-day hold is your only opportunity to negotiate a release. After the 21 days, the money is gone permanently.

How to Release a Bank Levy in Time

  • Set up an Installment Agreement: Once the IRS formally approves a payment plan, they are legally required to release the levy. This is the fastest path for most taxpayers.
  • Submit an Offer in Compromise: Filing an OIC immediately suspends all collection activity — including the bank levy — while the application is under review.
  • Prove Economic Hardship: If the levy is preventing you from meeting basic living expenses (food, housing, utilities), the IRS must release it. File Form 911 through the Taxpayer Advocate Service for emergency intervention.
  • Request a Collection Due Process Hearing: If you never received proper notice, you can contest the levy and get an immediate suspension.

What the IRS Cannot Levy

  • SSI (Supplemental Security Income)
  • Workers' compensation payments
  • Certain unemployment benefits
  • Child support and alimony payments received
  • Minimum wage equivalent in paycheck (for wage levies)
