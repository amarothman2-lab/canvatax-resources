What Is the IRS Hardship Program?

The IRS Hardship Program — formally known as Currently Not Collectible (CNC) status — is a relief designation that freezes all IRS collection activity when paying your tax debt would prevent you from meeting basic living expenses. It is not widely advertised, but it is a real, legal, and commonly used program.

What "Hardship" Means to the IRS

The IRS defines hardship as: your allowable monthly living expenses (based on national and local standards for housing, food, transportation, and healthcare) equal or exceed your monthly income. In other words, you have zero disposable income after necessary expenses.

What the IRS Hardship Program Does

  • Stops all wage garnishments and bank levies immediately
  • Prevents new enforcement actions from being issued
  • Requires zero monthly payments while your account is in hardship status
  • The 10-year collection statute continues running during CNC — meaning your balance can eventually expire

Who Qualifies?

Common qualifying situations include: unemployment or very low income, serious medical issues consuming all available income, recent job loss or business closure, living on Social Security or disability benefits only, significant housing costs relative to income.

The Application Process

You'll need Form 433-A (individuals) or 433-F (simplified). The IRS reviews your income sources, monthly expenses, bank account balances, and assets. CNC is typically approved within 2–8 weeks. For emergency situations — an active garnishment or levy — the IRS can grant temporary hardship holds over the phone while you prepare the full documentation.

After CNC Approval

The IRS reviews CNC status periodically — typically every 1–2 years. If your income improves significantly, you'll be transitioned out. In the meantime, CanvaTax monitors your case and evaluates when an Offer in Compromise may be a better long-term strategy. Free consultation: (858) 610-9779.
