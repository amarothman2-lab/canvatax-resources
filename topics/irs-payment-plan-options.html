IRS Installment Agreement Options

An IRS installment agreement (payment plan) lets you pay your tax debt in monthly payments over time. Once in a payment plan, the IRS suspends most collection activity — no new levies, no new garnishments — as long as you stay current.

Types of IRS Payment Plans

  • Short-Term Payment Plan (120 days): For balances under $100,000. No setup fee. Available online at IRS.gov. Best if you can pay in full within 4 months.
  • Long-Term Streamlined Agreement (under $50,000): Pay over up to 72 months. Setup fee: $31 (online/Direct Debit) or $107 (phone/mail). No financial disclosure required. Set up online in minutes.
  • Non-Streamlined Agreement (over $50,000): Requires Form 433-A or 433-F financial disclosure. IRS analyzes income, expenses, and assets to determine payment amount. More complex — professional help strongly recommended.
  • Partial Pay Installment Agreement (PPIA): You pay less than the standard amount because you genuinely can't afford full payments. The IRS reviews your finances every 2 years. Any remaining balance when the 10-year collection statute expires is written off.

How the IRS Calculates Your Payment

For streamlined agreements (under $50,000), it's simple: balance ÷ 72 months = your minimum payment. For non-streamlined, the IRS subtracts allowable living expenses from your monthly income. The difference is your required payment — which can be significantly reduced with proper documentation.

Benefits of a Payment Plan

  • Stops wage garnishment and bank levies immediately
  • Reduces failure-to-pay penalty from 0.5%/month to 0.25%/month
  • Under $25,000 + Direct Debit = IRS withdraws public tax lien filing
  • Buys time to improve finances and pursue other options (OIC, etc.)
