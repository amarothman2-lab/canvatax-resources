IRS Tax Debt Relief for Texas Taxpayers

Texas has no state income tax — but IRS federal tax debt is just as real for Texas residents as anywhere in the country. Texas taxpayers also frequently deal with business tax issues, self-employment tax debt, and payroll tax problems given the state's strong entrepreneurial culture.

Texas Advantages in IRS Negotiations

Texas has some of the most debtor-friendly asset protection laws in the country. Your homestead (primary residence) is protected from most creditors — but NOT from the IRS. The IRS can file a federal tax lien against a Texas homestead, although seizing and selling it requires court approval and is extremely rare. Texas retirement accounts also have strong protections from general creditors, but again, the IRS can reach them in extreme cases.

IRS Programs Available to Texas Taxpayers

  • Offer in Compromise: Texas residents with limited income and assets frequently qualify for significant settlements — often 10–25 cents on the dollar.
  • Installment Agreement: Streamlined plans for debts under $50,000 set up online without financial disclosure.
  • Currently Not Collectible: Self-employed Texans with irregular income often qualify during slow periods.
  • Penalty Abatement: First-Time Abatement eliminates penalties for qualifying Texas filers who missed one year.

Small Business Payroll Tax in Texas

Texas has a large small business economy. Payroll tax (941) debt is one of the most common IRS issues we see from Texas clients — especially in industries like construction, restaurants, and retail. The Trust Fund Recovery Penalty makes owners personally liable. Act fast before the IRS assigns a Revenue Officer.
